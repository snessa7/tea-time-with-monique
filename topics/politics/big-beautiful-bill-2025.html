The One Big Beautiful Bill

House Resolution 1 - The One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025

Passed House 215-214 | Now in Senate
$3.8 TRILLION

Projected addition to federal debt over 10 years

üí∞ Tax Provisions

2017 Tax Cuts Made Permanent
The bill locks in the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act provisions that were set to expire after 2025. This maintains the current seven tax brackets with the top rate at 37% instead of reverting to 39.6%. For a family earning $75,000, this saves approximately $1,700 annually.
No Tax on Tips, Overtime & Car Loans
Workers in hospitality and service industries won't pay federal income tax on tip income. Overtime pay becomes tax-free, potentially saving workers up to $1,750 annually. Interest on auto loans becomes deductible, helping millions of Americans afford vehicles.
Small Business Expansion
The Section 199A deduction increases from 20% to 23% and becomes permanent. This is projected to create over 1 million new jobs and generate $750 billion in economic growth. Businesses can immediately expense 100% of equipment purchases and R&D costs.
SALT Deduction Quadrupled
State and local tax deductions increase from $10,000 to $40,000 in 2025, with annual 1% increases through 2033. However, this phases out for incomes above $500,000, primarily benefiting middle and upper-middle class taxpayers in high-tax states.
Enhanced Child Tax Credit
The credit increases from $2,000 to $2,500 per child for 2025-2028, then permanently stays at $2,000. This provides additional relief for over 40 million families, putting an extra $500 per child in their pockets during the enhanced period.
Senior Tax Relief
Seniors receive an expanded standard deduction, saving up to $450 annually. While not eliminating taxes on Social Security as originally promised, this provides meaningful relief for retirees on fixed incomes.

üéÅ New Programs

Trump Accounts for Children
Every child born in America receives a $1,000 one-time federal deposit into a special savings account. These "Trump Accounts" (originally MAGA Accounts) can grow tax-free and be used for education, first home purchases, or starting a business when the child reaches adulthood.
Supercharged Health Savings Accounts
HSA contribution limits double for middle-income earners: $8,600 for individuals and $17,100 for couples (earning under $75k/$150k). Revolutionary: HSAs can now cover gym memberships and fitness classes up to $500/year for individuals or $1,000 for families.
Infrastructure Modernization
$12.5 billion allocated to modernize the FAA's antiquated systems, replacing floppy disks and paper strips with 21st-century technology. This addresses critical air traffic control staffing shortages affecting 27,000 daily flights.

Medicaid Reductions
Significant cuts to Medicaid funding will affect millions of low-income Americans' access to healthcare. States will need to fill funding gaps or reduce coverage, potentially impacting nursing home care, children's health programs, and disability services.
SNAP Benefits Slashed
Food stamp programs face what critics call the "biggest cut in the program's history". This will reduce assistance for millions of families struggling with food insecurity, potentially affecting children's nutrition and school performance.
ACA Premium Subsidies Expire
Affordable Care Act premium tax credits end after 2025, causing out-of-pocket insurance costs to spike. Millions may lose coverage as premiums become unaffordable, particularly affecting middle-income families who don't qualify for Medicaid.
Education Funding Cuts
Pell Grants and other education programs face reductions, limiting college access for low-income students. This reverses decades of investment in making higher education accessible and may force students to take on more debt.

üîß Other Major Provisions

Green Energy Incentives Eliminated
All tax credits for electric vehicles, solar panels, and renewable energy investments are cancelled. This reverses Biden-era climate policies and may slow the transition to clean energy, affecting both consumers and the growing green tech industry.
Border & Military Spending Boost
Significant increases in border security funding and military spending aim to strengthen national defense and immigration enforcement. Details on specific allocations are still being finalized in committee.
Debt Ceiling Raised
The federal debt limit increases by $4 trillion, preventing a potential default crisis this summer. This accommodation is necessary given the bill's projected $3.8 trillion addition to the national debt.
Hidden Provisions
Critics note concerning additions: courts can't hold officials in contempt, the $200 tax on gun silencers is repealed, and various regulatory rollbacks are embedded throughout the 1,100-page document.

This overview is based on the House-passed version. The Senate is expected to make significant changes.
